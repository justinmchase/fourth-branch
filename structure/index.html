Structure

  • The Fourth Branch shall establish a central corporation known as The National Corporation of the United States of America.
  • The National Corporation shall operate like a corporation in all respects except it shall be exempt from taxation.
  • The Board of Directors functions similarly to a corporate board of directors but within the government.
  • The board is accountable to the American people through a democratic process.

Federally Elected Officials

  • Seats are filled with federally elected officials to ensure democratic representation.
  • All members of the Fourth Branch would be elected at the federal level.
  • These elections could occur in a manner similar to Congressional elections but would be specific to this branch.

Board Size and Composition

  • To effectively carry out its duties, the Fourth Branch would have 21 board members.
  • This size provides balance between broad representation and efficient governance.
  • It enables adequate representation for a range of political parties, reflecting a multi-party electoral system.
  • The odd number of board members helps to prevent voting deadlocks, ensuring that decision-making processes are not stymied.
